Question 1¶

What are the earliest, most recent, most common year of birth (only available for NYC and Chicago)?

In [5]:
# Function to find earliest birth year 
find.min.birthyear = function(table){
    result = min(table$Birth.Year, na.rm = TRUE)
    return(result)
}

# Function to find latest birth year
find.max.birthyear = function(table){
    result = max(table$Birth.Year, na.rm = TRUE)
    return(result)
}

# Function to find most common birth year
find.av.birthyear = function(table){
    result = mean(table$Birth.Year, na.rm = TRUE)
    return(result)
}
In [6]:
# Displaying the earliest, most recent, and most common birth years from the New York table

ny.min.birthyear = find.min.birthyear(ny)
ny.max.birthyear = find.max.birthyear(ny)
ny.av.birthyear = find.av.birthyear(ny)
paste('New York earliest birth year, most recent, and most common:', round(ny.min.birthyear, digits=2),',', round(ny.max.birthyear, digits=2),',',round(ny.av.birthyear, digits=0))
'New York earliest birth year, most recent, and most common: 1885 , 2001 , 1978'

The earliest birth years were found through finding the minimum birth years in each of the cities. The most recent birth years were found by searching for the maximum birth years in each city, while the most common birth years were found through searching the mean birth year in each city. Based on the data found above and shown below, we can see that the earliest birth years for New York and Chicago as being the years 1885 and 1899. Since these people would be over a hundred years old, I am guessing that this is data that needs to be cleaned a little bit more. The most recent birth years in New York and Chicago are the years 2001 and 2002. While the most common birth years that use this biking system for travel in these two cities are 1978 and 1981. This means that the most common age for rental bikers in New York is 39, while the most common in Chicago is 36.

find.mean = function(table){

find.minutes = function(seconds){

}

Question 3¶

What are the counts of each gender (only available for NYC and Chicago)?

In [15]:
# Function to return female count
female.count = function(table){
    count = nrow(subset(table, table$Gender == 'Female'))
    return(count)
}

# Function to return male count
male.count = function(table){
    count = nrow(subset(table, table$Gender == 'Male'))
    return(count)
}
In [16]:
# Finding the gender counts of New York

ny.females = female.count(ny)
ny.males = male.count(ny)
paste('Number of females in New York table:', ny.females, ', number of males in New York table:', ny.males)
'Number of females in New York table: 12159 , number of males in New York table: 37201'

'Number of females in Chicago table: 1723 , number of males in Chicago table: 5159'

The numbers of each gender within each city were found through counting the number of rows where gender was equal to either male or female. We can see from the data displayed above that the number of females that rented bikes in New York was 12,159, while the number of males was 37,201. Therefore, we can see that males are more likely to rent a bicycle in New York than females. In Chicago, the number of females that rented a bike was 1,723, while the number of males was 5,159. Chicago also had a greater number of bike rentals from males, however, New York has a higher number of rentals in total.
